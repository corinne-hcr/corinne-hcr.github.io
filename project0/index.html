Becoming Friends with Your Camera — Your Name

Part 1: Selfie

Discussion

In the first image, where the camera is held very close to my mother’s face, the wide field of view exaggerates features nearest to the lens (e.g., nose and forehead) and shrinks features farther away. When the camera is moved back and zoomed in, the perspective is flattened and the relative size difference between near and far features is reduced, so her facial proportions look more natural.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression (with Grey)

Discussion

This is Grey. In the wide-angle images, Grey appears small in the foreground while the building and handrails stretch dramatically into the background, creating a strong sense of depth. As the focal length increases, Grey occupies more of the frame and the building seems to shift forward, appearing much closer behind him.
